Success Stories

The following cases are drawn from public reports by organizations such as Human Rights Watch, Amnesty International, and major news outlets. These real cases illustrate the tragic consequences of honor crime leniency in Jordan. Due to the sensitive nature of the subject, some details have been modified or redacted to protect the privacy of victims and their families.

Kifaya Husayn

What Happened: In 1994, 16-year-old Kifaya was fatally slashed in the throat and strangled by her older brother after a social media conversation was deemed dishonorable by her family.

Perpetrator: Her older brother.

Reason: Accused of tarnishing family honor through perceived immoral behavior.

Sentence: Initially sentenced to 15 years, later reduced to 7½ years on appeal.

Ahlam (Surname Redacted)

What Happened: In 2020, Ahlam, a woman reportedly in her late 30s, was brutally beaten to death by her father on a public street following a dispute over her personal freedom.

Perpetrator: Her father.

Reason: The act was justified by the family as necessary to protect their honor.

Sentence: The perpetrator was arrested; reports indicate a light sentence pending trial.

Aya Mahmoud

What Happened: Aya, a 19-year-old, was severely beaten to death by her brother after being accused of engaging in inappropriate contact with a male friend.

Perpetrator: Her brother.

Reason: Alleged violation of family honor.

Sentence: The brother received a minimal sentence of 6 months.

Fatima Qassem

What Happened: In a widely reported 2007 case, Fatima was fatally stabbed by her brother after leaving home without permission.

Perpetrator: Her brother.

Reason: The killing was defended as a measure to restore family honor.

Sentence: The perpetrator was controversially given a 3-month sentence.

Zainab Al-Farouq

What Happened: A 14-year-old girl, Zainab was stabbed to death after her social media activities raised suspicions of improper conduct.

Perpetrator: A relative (name redacted).

Reason: Cited as necessary to eliminate dishonor.

Sentence: Reports indicate an exceptionally light sentence of around 4 months.

Layla Ibrahim

What Happened: Layla was fatally stabbed by her cousin after attending a mixed-gender school, which her family claimed violated traditional norms.

Perpetrator: Her cousin.

Reason: Her modern educational pursuits were labeled as dishonorable.

Sentence: The cousin received a 6-month sentence.

Mariam Hassan

What Happened: Mariam was fatally beaten by her father after defying expectations by pursuing an independent career.

Perpetrator: Her father.

Reason: Her ambitions were seen as a betrayal of family honor.

Sentence: He served 8 months (recorded as “time served”).

Hana Qasim

What Happened: Hana was fatally beaten and strangled by her brother after being seen with a male friend.

Perpetrator: Her brother.

Reason: Claimed necessary to restore the family’s reputation.

Sentence: He received a 6-month sentence.

Salma Youssef

What Happened: Salma was fatally beaten by her father after expressing her wish to pursue studies abroad.

Perpetrator: Her father.

Reason: Her ambitions were viewed as a challenge to traditional values.

Sentence: The father was given a 9-month sentence.

Zeina Qassem

What Happened: Zeina, a high school student, was fatally stabbed by her uncle after rumors of an illicit relationship emerged in her community.

Perpetrator: Her uncle.

Reason: The killing was defended as essential to preserve family honor.

Sentence: The uncle received a 4-month sentence.
